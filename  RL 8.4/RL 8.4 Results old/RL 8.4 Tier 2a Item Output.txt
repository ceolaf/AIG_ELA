1. In paragraph 1, the town of Hanover is described as “anchored on a windy Nebraska tableland” and the buildings are said to be “huddled on the gray prairie.” How do these word choices most strongly affect the tone of the description?

A. They make the town seem cheerful and welcoming, like a safe harbor from the storm.  
B. They make the town seem small and fragile, as if it could be swept away by the wind at any moment.*  
C. They make the town seem powerful and important, firmly rooted in the landscape.  
D. They make the town seem mysterious and magical, separated from the real world.

**Correct Answer: B***

---

### Rationales

**Option B (Correct):**  
- The verb “anchored” usually suggests something held in place against strong forces, like a ship in a storm.  
- Here, it is paired with “windy” and later with buildings that “huddled” and seemed to have no “appearance of permanence.”  
- The figurative language makes the town feel as if it is barely holding on, small and vulnerable to the harsh environment.  
- This creates a tone of fragility and precariousness, which matches the idea that the town is “trying not to be blown away.”

**Option A (Distractor):**  
- “Anchored” can sometimes suggest safety, but the rest of the description contradicts a cheerful, welcoming mood: “windy,” “gray prairie,” “gray sky,” “low drab buildings,” and “howling wind.”  
- “Huddled” implies fear or exposure, not comfort.  
- A student choosing this might focus only on the positive connotation of “anchored” and ignore the negative details that create a bleak tone.

**Option C (Distractor):**  
- Words like “powerful” and “important” clash with the passage’s emphasis on the town’s lack of “permanence” and the way buildings look like they are “straying off by themselves.”  
- The town does not seem “firmly rooted”; instead, it seems as if it might be blown away.  
- A student might misread “anchored” as a sign of strength without considering the rest of the imagery.

**Option D (Distractor):**  
- Nothing in “anchored,” “windy Nebraska tableland,” or “huddled on the gray prairie” suggests magic or mystery. The description is realistic and harsh.  
- The tone is bleak and exposed, not fantastical.  
- A student choosing this might be reacting to the vivid imagery but mislabeling the tone as “mysterious” rather than harsh and precarious.

2. In the sentence, “He was a little country boy, and this village was to him a very strange and perplexing place, where people wore fine clothes and had hard hearts,” what is the effect of the phrase “had hard hearts” on the meaning and tone of the passage?

A. It suggests that the people in the village are physically strong and healthy.  
B. It shows that the boy believes the townspeople are cold and unkind toward him.*  
C. It means the townspeople are brave and not easily scared.  
D. It implies that the villagers are strict but secretly caring.

**Correct Answer: B***

---

### Rationales

**Option B (Correct):**  
- “Hard hearts” is a figurative phrase; it does not mean their hearts are literally hard.  
- In context, the boy finds the village “strange and perplexing” and feels “shy and awkward” and afraid people will laugh at him.  
- Calling the people “hard-hearted” conveys his sense that they are cold, unfeeling, and unkind.  
- This word choice strengthens a tone of isolation and emotional harshness from the boy’s point of view.

**Option A (Distractor):**  
- “Hard hearts” does not refer to physical strength or health; it is about emotional qualities.  
- A student might confuse “hard” with “strong,” but the context (his fear and discomfort) shows it describes their lack of kindness, not their bodies.

**Option C (Distractor):**  
- Bravery or lack of fear is not suggested here.  
- The passage focuses on how the boy feels judged and out of place, not on whether the townspeople are courageous.  
- A student might think “hard” means “tough,” but the emotional context points instead to coldness.

**Option D (Distractor):**  
- Nothing in the sentence suggests “secretly caring.”  
- The phrase “hard hearts” carries a negative connotation; it does not hint at hidden kindness.  
- A student might assume adults are strict-but-kind, but this contradicts the boy’s perception in the text.
